About Brain Tumors

A brain tumor is an abnormal growth of cells inside the brain or surrounding tissues. Early diagnosis plays a critical role in treatment success.

Definition: A brain tumor occurs when abnormal cells grow uncontrollably within the brain or nearby structures. Tumors can be primary (originating in the brain) or secondary (metastatic, spreading from other organs).

Types of Brain Tumors:
• Gliomas: Develop from glial cells and include astrocytoma, oligodendroglioma, and glioblastoma.
• Meningiomas: Arise from the meninges; usually benign and slow-growing.
• Pituitary Tumors: Affect hormone production and gland function.
• Pineal Tumors: Rare tumors near the pineal gland.

Tumors are graded by the World Health Organization (Grade I–IV) based on aggressiveness. Recent research shows that deep learning models applied to MRI images can classify tumor types with high accuracy, supporting faster diagnosis and treatment planning.

Signs & Symptoms

Symptoms depend on tumor size, type, and location within the brain. Some symptoms develop gradually, while others appear suddenly.

Common signs and symptoms of brain tumors include persistent or worsening headaches, especially in the morning, seizures, nausea or vomiting, blurred or double vision, and difficulty with balance or coordination. Patients may also experience weakness or numbness in the arms or legs, speech difficulties, memory problems, confusion, personality changes, or hearing loss. Because these symptoms can worsen over time, early medical evaluation is essential for effective treatment.

How the System Works

NeuroVision uses artificial intelligence to assist doctors in brain tumor detection through MRI analysis.

The NeuroVision system allows users to upload MRI brain images through a secure web interface. The uploaded image undergoes preprocessing such as resizing and normalization before being passed to a trained deep learning model based on a convolutional neural network (VGG). The model analyzes the MRI scan to detect abnormal patterns and predicts whether a tumor is present. The system outputs the predicted tumor class along with a confidence score, helping clinicians make informed diagnostic decisions. This approach reduces manual effort, speeds up diagnosis, and provides AI-assisted clinical support.
